This guide includes **four rye sourdough recipes**:

1. Rustic rye sourdough loaf
2. Rye sourdough baked in a loaf pan
3. Soft rye sandwich bread
4. Classic deli-style rye bread

Each recipe builds on the previous one but is optimized for a different baking style.

---

# Rustic Rye Sourdough Loaf {#rustic-rye}

A classic artisan rye sourdough with a chewy crust and slightly dense crumb.

## Levain (Preferment) {#rustic-levain}

Mix:

* 30 g sourdough starter
* 60 g water
* 60 g rye flour

Ferment **4–6 hours** until bubbly.

## Dough Ingredients {#rustic-ingredients}

* 120 g active starter
* 300 g bread flour
* 150 g rye flour
* 330 g water
* 10 g salt

Optional:

* 1 tbsp caraway seeds

## Mix {#rustic-mix}

Combine starter, water, bread flour, and rye flour.

Mix until no dry flour remains.

Rest **30 minutes**.

Add salt and mix.

## Bulk Fermentation {#rustic-bulk}

Ferment **3–4 hours**.

Perform **stretch-and-folds every 30 minutes** during the first two hours.

## Shape {#rustic-shape}

Shape into a **boule** and place in a floured banneton.

## Proof {#rustic-proof}

Proof:

* **1–2 hours room temperature**, or
* **8–16 hours refrigerated**

## Bake {#rustic-bake}

Preheat Dutch oven to **475°F (245°C)**.

Bake:

* **20 minutes covered**
* **20–25 minutes uncovered at 450°F (232°C)**

Cool at least **1 hour** before slicing.

# Rye Sourdough Loaf (Loaf Pan Version) {#pan-rye}

This variation uses a **loaf pan**, making rye bread easier to shape.

## Ingredients {#pan-ingredients}

* 120 g sourdough starter
* 300 g bread flour
* 150 g rye flour
* 330 g water
* 10 g salt

Optional:

* 1 tbsp caraway seeds

## Mix {#pan-mix}

Mix starter, water, and flours until combined.

Rest **30 minutes**.

Add salt and mix.

## Bulk Fermentation {#pan-bulk}

Ferment **3–4 hours**, performing stretch-and-folds during the first two hours.

## Shape for Pan {#pan-shape}

Shape dough into a **tight log**.

Place seam-side down in a **greased loaf pan (8.5 × 4.5 or 9 × 5)**.

## Proof {#pan-proof}

Allow dough to rise until it reaches **about 1 inch below the top of the pan**.

## Bake {#pan-bake}

Bake at **450°F (232°C)** for **40–45 minutes**.

Cool completely before slicing.

# Rye Sourdough Sandwich Bread {#sandwich-rye}

A softer rye bread designed for **sandwich slices**.

## Ingredients {#sandwich-ingredients}

* 120 g sourdough starter
* 280 g bread flour
* 170 g rye flour
* 330 g water
* 20 g honey or sugar
* 20 g neutral oil or melted butter
* 10 g salt

Optional:

* 1 tbsp caraway seeds

## Mix {#sandwich-mix}

Combine starter, water, flours, honey, and oil.

Mix until combined.

Rest **30 minutes**.

Add salt and mix.

## Bulk Fermentation {#sandwich-bulk}

Ferment **3–4 hours**, performing stretch-and-folds every 30 minutes during the first two hours.

## Shape {#sandwich-shape}

Shape dough into a **log**.

Place into a greased loaf pan.

## Proof {#sandwich-proof}

Allow dough to rise until **level with the top of the pan**.

## Bake {#sandwich-bake}

Bake at **375°F (190°C)** for **40–45 minutes**.

Remove from pan immediately and cool.

# Deli-Style Rye Bread {#deli-rye}

A classic rye used for **pastrami and corned beef sandwiches**.

## Ingredients {#deli-ingredients}

* 120 g sourdough starter
* 320 g bread flour
* 130 g rye flour
* 320 g water
* 20 g honey or sugar
* 20 g oil or melted butter
* 10 g salt
* 1 tbsp caraway seeds

Optional (traditional deli flavor):

* 1 tsp cocoa powder
* 1 tsp instant coffee

## Mix {#deli-mix}

Combine starter, water, flours, honey, oil, and optional cocoa/coffee.

Mix until combined.

Rest **30 minutes**.

Add salt and caraway seeds.

## Bulk Fermentation {#deli-bulk}

Ferment **3–4 hours** with stretch-and-folds during the first two hours.

## Shape {#deli-shape}

Shape dough into a **tight log**.

Place in a greased loaf pan.

## Proof {#deli-proof}

Proof until dough rises **near the rim of the pan**.

## Bake {#deli-bake}

Bake at **375°F (190°C)** for **40–45 minutes**.

If crust browns too quickly, tent with foil during the final 10 minutes.

## Optional Deli Finish {#deli-finish}

For a traditional deli crust:

Brush the loaf with **water or cornstarch glaze** during the final minutes of baking.

Optional topping:

* sprinkle additional **caraway seeds**

---

# Baking Notes {#notes}

* Rye dough is naturally **stickier than wheat dough**.
* Allow bread to **cool fully before slicing**.
* Cold fermentation improves flavor and texture.
* Caraway seeds give rye bread its traditional flavor but are optional.

Happy baking.

There are lemon cakes, and then there’s *this* lemon cake. Dense, rich, unapologetically moist, and loaded with real lemon flavor—not just sweetness with a hint of citrus. This is the kind of slice that feels substantial in your hand, with a tight crumb, a bright lemon aroma, and that signature thick glaze that cracks just slightly when you bite into it.

What you’re getting here are **two versions**:
- The classic Starbucks-style loaf (soft, moist, balanced)
- A **bakery-style dense version** (heavier, tighter crumb, almost cheesecake-adjacent)

Both deliver that bold lemon punch—but the second leans into indulgence.

---

## 🍋 Classic Starbucks-Style Lemon Pound Cake

### Ingredients

**Cake**
- 1 ½ cups all-purpose flour  
- 1 tsp baking powder  
- ½ tsp baking soda  
- ½ tsp salt  
- 3 large eggs  
- 1 cup granulated sugar  
- 2 tbsp butter, melted  
- 0.5 cup vegetable oil  
- 2 tbsp fresh lemon juice  
- 1 tbsp lemon zest  
- 1 tsp vanilla extract  
- 0.5 cup sour cream (full-fat preferred)

**Glaze**
- 1 cup powdered sugar  
- 2–3 tbsp fresh lemon juice  
- 0.5 tsp lemon zest (optional)

---

### Instructions

1. **Prep**
   - Preheat oven to 350°F  
   - Grease or line an 8x4 or 9x5 loaf pan  

2. **Dry Ingredients**
   - Whisk flour, baking powder, baking soda, and salt  

3. **Wet Ingredients**
   - Whisk eggs and sugar until pale and slightly thick  
   - Add melted butter, oil, lemon juice, zest, vanilla, and sour cream  
   - Mix until smooth  

4. **Combine**
   - Fold dry ingredients into wet mixture (do not overmix)  

5. **Bake**
   - Pour into pan  
   - Bake 45–55 minutes until a toothpick comes out clean  

6. **Cool**
   - Cool in pan for 10 minutes, then transfer to rack  

---

### Glaze

- Mix powdered sugar and lemon juice until thick but pourable  
- Pour over fully cooled loaf  
- Let set before slicing

## 🍰 Bakery-Style Dense Lemon Pound Cake (Heavier Crumb)

This version pushes toward that **tight, rich, almost velvety density** you’d expect from a high-end bakery slice.

### Key Differences
- More fat (butter + sour cream)  
- Slightly less leavening  
- Lower liquid ratio for tighter crumb  
- Longer, gentler bake  

---

### Ingredients

**Cake**
- 1 ½ cups all-purpose flour  
- ¾ tsp baking powder  
- ¼ tsp baking soda  
- ½ tsp salt  
- 3 large eggs  
- 1 cup granulated sugar  
- 6 tbsp butter, melted  
- ⅓ cup vegetable oil  
- 2 tbsp fresh lemon juice  
- 1 ½ tbsp lemon zest  
- 1 tsp vanilla extract  
- ¾ cup sour cream (full-fat)

**Optional (for extra density)**
- 2 tbsp cream cheese, softened and blended into wet mix  

**Glaze**
- 1 cup powdered sugar  
- 2–3 tbsp fresh lemon juice  
- 0.5 tsp lemon zest (optional)

---

### Instructions

1. **Prep**
   - Preheat oven to 325°F  
   - Line loaf pan  

2. **Dry Ingredients**
   - Whisk flour, baking powder, baking soda, and salt  

3. **Wet Ingredients**
   - Whisk eggs and sugar thoroughly until smooth (not fluffy)  
   - Add melted butter, oil, lemon juice, zest, vanilla  
   - Mix in sour cream (and cream cheese if using) until fully incorporated  

4. **Combine**
   - Fold in dry ingredients gently  
   - Batter will be thicker than the classic version  

5. **Bake**
   - Pour into pan and smooth top  
   - Bake 55–70 minutes  
   - Tent with foil if browning too quickly  

6. **Cool Slowly**
   - Let cool in pan 15–20 minutes before removing  

---

### Glaze

- Mix powdered sugar and lemon juice until **thick and opaque**  
- Pour generously over fully cooled loaf  

---

## Notes

- Zest lemons before juicing for best flavor  
- Do not overmix once flour is added  
- Full-fat sour cream is critical for texture  
- Flavor improves significantly after resting overnight  
- Refrigeration will make the dense version even tighter and richer
